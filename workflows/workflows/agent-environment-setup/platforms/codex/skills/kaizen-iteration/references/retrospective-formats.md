# Retrospective Formats Reference

Load this when facilitating a retrospective or choosing a format for the team.

---

## Choosing a Format

| Format | Best For | Team Size | Duration |
|--------|---------|-----------|----------|
| Start-Stop-Continue | Quick, action-oriented retros | 3-8 | 30 min |
| 4Ls | Reflective teams; first retros | 4-12 | 45 min |
| Sailboat | Visual thinkers; goal-oriented teams | 4-10 | 45 min |
| Mad-Sad-Glad | Emotionally safe exploration | 3-8 | 30 min |
| Timeline | After long projects or incidents | 5-15 | 60 min |

---

## Format 1: Start-Stop-Continue

### Structure

Three columns:

| Start (begin doing) | Stop (cease doing) | Continue (keep doing) |
|---------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| What should we start? | What should we stop? | What is working? |

### Facilitation Guide

1. **Generate (5 min)** -- Each person writes 2-3 sticky notes per column silently.
2. **Group (5 min)** -- Place notes on the board. Cluster similar themes.
3. **Discuss (15 min)** -- Vote on top 3 items. Discuss root causes for each.
4. **Action (5 min)** -- Convert top items into improvement experiments with owners.

### When It Works Best

- The team is experienced with retros and wants to move fast.
- There are clear process pain points that need immediate action.
- The facilitator wants to limit discussion time.

### Common Pitfall

"Continue" items get no discussion time. Acknowledging what works is important for morale. Spend at least 2 minutes celebrating the "Continue" column.

## Format 2: 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For)

### Structure

Four quadrants:

| Liked | Learned |
|-------|---------|
| What went well? | What did we learn? |

| Lacked | Longed For |
|--------|-----------|
| What was missing? | What do we wish we had? |

### Facilitation Guide

1. **Generate (8 min)** -- Each person writes 1-2 notes per quadrant silently.
2. **Share (10 min)** -- Each person shares their top item from each quadrant.
3. **Cluster (5 min)** -- Group related items across quadrants.
4. **Prioritize (5 min)** -- Dot-vote on the most impactful clusters.
5. **Action (10 min)** -- Design improvement experiments for top 2 clusters.

### When It Works Best

- New teams running their first retrospective.
- After a complex project with many learning opportunities.
- When the team needs emotional safety (the "Liked" and "Learned" quadrants balance negativity).

## Format 3: Sailboat

### Structure

Visual metaphor:

```
                    🏝️ Island (Goal)
                   /
    ☀️ Wind (Helping) →  ⛵ Boat (Team)  ← ⚓ Anchor (Holding back)
                   \
                    🪨 Rocks (Risks)
```

- **Island:** Where we want to be (sprint/project goal).
- **Wind:** What is pushing us forward.
- **Anchor:** What is holding us back.
- **Rocks:** Risks ahead that could damage us.

### Facilitation Guide

1. **Set the island (3 min)** -- Define the goal the team is sailing toward.
2. **Generate (8 min)** -- Notes for wind, anchors, and rocks.
3. **Discuss winds (5 min)** -- Celebrate and reinforce what helps.
4. **Discuss anchors (10 min)** -- Root cause analysis on what holds the team back.
5. **Discuss rocks (5 min)** -- Risk assessment and mitigation planning.
6. **Action (7 min)** -- Convert anchors and rocks into experiments.

## Format 4: Mad-Sad-Glad

### Structure

Three emotional columns:

| Mad (frustrated) | Sad (disappointed) | Glad (happy) |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| What angered us? | What disappointed us? | What made us happy? |

### When It Works Best

- After a difficult sprint with team conflict or burnout.
- When the team needs to process emotions before problem-solving.
- When previous retros felt too clinical and missed the human element.

### Facilitation Tip

Mad-Sad-Glad surfaces emotions, which is valuable. But emotions need to be converted into actionable observations. After the sharing phase, ask: "What process or system change would reduce this frustration?"

## Format 5: Timeline

### Structure

A horizontal timeline of the sprint or project, with events plotted above (positive) or below (negative) the line:

```
Good  +  ✓ Feature A shipped    ✓ Great pairing session
      |        |                       |
------+--------+---------+-------------+--------→ Time
      |                  |
Bad   -        ✗ Prod incident    ✗ Spec rework
```

### When It Works Best

- After multi-week projects or lengthy sprints.
- When the team needs to reconstruct what happened before analyzing it.
- When sequence matters (e.g., "the incident happened BECAUSE the deploy was rushed BECAUSE the deadline was moved").

## Anti-Patterns

### The Complaint Session

**Symptom:** Retro becomes a venting session with no actionable outcomes.
**Fix:** Set a ground rule: every complaint must be paired with a proposed experiment.

### The Status Update

**Symptom:** Team members report what they did instead of what they learned.
**Fix:** Ban descriptions of work done. Only allow observations about process, tools, and collaboration.

### The Blame Game

**Symptom:** Observations name individuals as the cause of problems.
**Fix:** Use the "Prime Directive": assume everyone did the best they could given what they knew at the time. Focus on systems, not people.

### The Vanishing Action Items

**Symptom:** Experiments from the last retro were never started.
**Fix:** Begin every retro by reviewing the previous retro's action items. If items are consistently abandoned, the team is overcommitting.
